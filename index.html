Dashboard Pattern Detection Study

CS 526 — Computer Graphics II

About This Study

This study investigates how people detect patterns across multiple charts in dashboard layouts. You will see dashboard panels, each containing a 2×2 grid of charts showing four variables (V1, V2, V3, V4).

What You Will Do

Find the panel where the charts are related to each other, or indicate that all panels look the same.

Time

~25 minutes. Starts easy (2 panels) and gradually increases to 20. Warmup rounds include feedback.

Eligibility

Normal or corrected-to-normal vision. Desktop/laptop with screen ≥13 inches.

Consent

Participation is voluntary and anonymous. You may stop at any time. This study has been reviewed by the UIC Office for the Protection of Research Subjects (OPRS).

Background Information

Instructions

Each trial shows several dashboard panels. Every panel contains a 2×2 grid of 4 charts (V1, V2, V3, V4), each with 8–12 data points.

TARGET panel: The 4 variables are correlated — V1 and V2 move together (both up or both down), while V3 and V4 move in opposite directions. There is a visible relationship ACROSS the charts.

DISTRACTOR panels: Each chart looks normal on its own, but the 4 variables have NO relationship. They are independent.

Your job: Find the panel where the charts "move together." Click it.

NULL trials: Sometimes ALL panels are the same (no target). Click "All dashboards look the same".

Chart Types

Charts may be bar, line, dot, or scatter. Some panels use all the same type. Some panels use a mix of different types — for example, V1 as a bar chart, V2 as a line chart, V3 as a scatter plot, V4 as a dot plot. This reflects how real dashboards look.

Progressive Difficulty

2 panels → 4 panels → 10 panels → 20 panels. Warmup rounds include feedback so you can learn the task.
