Dashboard Pattern Detection Study

CS 526 — Computer Graphics II

About This Study

You will see dashboard panels, each containing a 2×2 grid of 4 charts (V1, V2, V3, V4). Your task is to find the panel where the charts are related to each other.

Time

About 10 minutes. Starts with two short warmup rounds with feedback, then scored rounds.

Eligibility

Normal or corrected-to-normal vision. Desktop or laptop with screen ≥13 inches.

Consent

Participation is voluntary and anonymous. You may stop at any time.

Background Information

Instructions

Each trial shows several dashboard panels. Every panel has a 2×2 grid of 4 charts (V1, V2, V3, V4).

What to look for:

In the TARGET panel, the charts are related — two of the variables move together, and the other two move in opposite directions. There is a visible pattern ACROSS the charts.

In all other panels, the charts have NO relationship. They are independent.

Your job: Find the panel where the charts seem connected. Click it.

If no panel stands out, click "All dashboards look the same."

Warmup First

You'll start with 2 panels, then 4 panels — both with feedback telling you if you're right or wrong. Then the real trials begin: 20 scored trials with 4 panels and 20 scored trials with 10 panels.
